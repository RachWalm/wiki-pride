1533

Buggery Act - UK

Described "buggery" as a "detestable and abominable vice" whether committed with "mankind or beast" punishable by death.

1540

First UK buggery execution

1st Baron of Hungerford executed and was also accused of treason and witchcraft

1835

Last UK execution

James Pratt and John Smith were two labourers and were hanged at Newgate Prison, London.

1861

Buggery no longer a capital crime - England

The punishment was reduced to life imprisonment in the Offences against person act.
